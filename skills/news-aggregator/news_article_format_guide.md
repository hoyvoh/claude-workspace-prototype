# News Article Format Guide

**Reference**: Based on [Grammarly's Guide to Writing a News Article](https://www.grammarly.com/blog/writing-tips/news-article/)

A professional news article requires specific structure, objectivity, and proper sourcing.

---

## Essential Components

### 1. Headline

- **Concise and compelling** (under 10 words ideal)
- **Active voice** preferred
- **Include key information** (who, what)
- **Factual, not sensational**

**Examples**:

- ✅ "Climate Summit Reaches Historic Agreement on Emissions"
- ✅ "Tech Giant Announces $10B Investment in AI Research"
- ❌ "You Won't Believe What Happened at Climate Summit!" (too clickbait)

### 2. Byline

- **Author name** and credentials
- **Publication date and time**
- **News organization**

**Format**:

```
By Jane Smith, Technology Reporter
Published: March 7, 2024 at 10:30 AM EST
Updated: March 7, 2024 at 2:15 PM EST
```

### 3. Lead (Lede)

- **First paragraph** answering the 5 W's and H
- **Who**: Key players involved
- **What**: The main event or development
- **When**: Timing of the event
- **Where**: Location
- **Why**: Reason or cause
- **How**: Method or process

**Example**:

> The United Nations Climate Summit in Geneva concluded Thursday with 195 countries agreeing to reduce carbon emissions by 50% by 2035, marking the most ambitious climate accord in history. The agreement, negotiated over two weeks, will require significant policy changes across major economies.

### 4. Body (Inverted Pyramid Structure)

**Most Important → Least Important**

#### Top Section (Critical Details)

- Expand on the lead
- Key facts and figures
- Immediate implications
- Direct quotes from primary sources

#### Middle Section (Supporting Information)

- Background context
- Related developments
- Expert analysis
- Additional quotes

#### Bottom Section (Supplementary Details)

- Historical context
- Related statistics
- Future implications
- Secondary sources

### 5. Quotes and Attribution

**Always attribute**:

- Direct quotes in quotation marks
- Source name and title
- Organization affiliation

**Format examples**:

```markdown
"This agreement represents a turning point in climate policy," said Dr. Maria Rodriguez, Chief Climate Scientist at the UN Environmental Programme.

According to the International Energy Agency, global carbon emissions reached a record 37 gigatons in 2023.

Rodriguez added that implementation challenges remain significant. "Countries must now translate commitments into concrete action," she emphasized.
```

### 6. Sourcing and References

**Types of sources**:

- **Primary**: Direct witnesses, participants, original documents
- **Secondary**: Expert a

nalysis, related organizations

- **Data**: Statistics, reports, studies

**Citation format**:

```markdown
The report, published by the World Economic Forum [1], indicates...

[1] World Economic Forum. (2024). "Global Climate Report 2024".
https://www.weforum.org/reports/climate-2024
```

### 7. Objectivity and Balance

**Maintain neutrality**:

- Present multiple perspectives
- Avoid loaded language
- Separate facts from opinions
- Clearly label analysis sections

**Examples**:

- ✅ "Supporters praised the agreement, while critics questioned its enforceability"
- ❌ "The amazing agreement was unfortunately criticized by skeptics"

### 8. Factual Accuracy

**Verify all information**:

- Cross-check facts with multiple sources
- Confirm spellings of names and places
- Verify numbers and statistics
- Link to original sources when possible

## Standard News Article Template

```markdown
# [Headline: Compelling, Factual, Active Voice]

**By [Author Name], [Title]**  
**Published**: [Date] at [Time] [Timezone]  
**Source**: [News Organization]

[LEAD PARAGRAPH: Answer 5 W's + H in 2-3 sentences]

[IMAGE: Relevant photo with caption]
_Caption: Brief description of image. Photo credit: [Source]_

## Key Points

- Bullet point 1: Major development
- Bullet point 2: Important fact
- Bullet point 3: Key implication

## [Section 1: Critical Details]

[Expand on lead with most important information]

"[Direct quote from primary source]," said [Name, Title, Organization].

[Additional critical facts]

## [Section 2: Context and Background]

[Provide necessary context for readers to understand significance]

According to [Source], [Supporting data or information].

## [Section 3: Reactions and Analysis]

[Multiple perspectives from stakeholders]

"[Quote from supporter]," commented [Name, Title].

However, [Name from opposing view] expressed concerns. "[Quote]," [he/she] stated.

## [Section 4: Implications]

[What this means going forward]

[Expert analysis with attribution]

## Additional Information

[Supplementary details, related developments, historical context]

---

**Related Articles:**

- [Link to related article 1]
- [Link to related article 2]

**Sources:**

1. [Source 1 with link]
2. [Source 2 with link]
3. [Source 3 with link]

**Tags:** [tag1], [tag2], [tag3]
```

## Quality Checklist

### Content

- [ ] Headline is clear, factual, and compelling
- [ ] Lead answers 5 W's + H
- [ ] Most important information first (inverted pyramid)
- [ ] Multiple sources cited
- [ ] Quotes properly attributed
- [ ] Facts verified
- [ ] Context provided

### Style

- [ ] Objective tone maintained
- [ ] Active voice used
- [ ] Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences)
- [ ] Simple, clear language
- [ ] No jargon without explanation
- [ ] Proper grammar and spelling

### Structure

- [ ] Clear sections with subheadings
- [ ] Logical flow of information
- [ ] Smooth transitions
- [ ] Related information grouped

### Ethics

- [ ] Multiple perspectives presented
- [ ] Potential conflicts of interest disclosed
- [ ] Corrections clearly marked if updated
- [ ] Sensitive topics handled appropriately

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Burying the lead**: Don't save the most important information for later

❌ **Passive voice**: "The bill was passed" → "Congress passed the bill"

❌ **Unattributed claims**: Always cite sources for facts and opinions

❌ **Editorializing**: Keep personal opinions out of straight news

❌ **Omitting opposing views**: Present multiple perspectives

❌ **Clickbait headlines**: Be compelling but factual

❌ **Unclear attribution**: Specify who said what

❌ **Mixing facts and analysis**: Clearly separate reporting from interpretation

## Example: Well-Formatted News Article

```markdown
# UN Climate Summit Achieves Historic Emissions Agreement

**By Sarah Johnson, Environment Correspondent**  
**Published**: March 7, 2024 at 3:45 PM GMT  
**Source**: Global News Wire

GENEVA — The United Nations Climate Summit concluded Thursday with 195 countries signing a landmark agreement to reduce global carbon emissions by 50% by 2035, marking the most ambitious climate accord in history.

![Climate Summit](https://example.com/summit.jpg)
_Delegates celebrate after reaching the agreement. Photo: UN Climate/Maria Santos_

## Key Points

- 195 countries committed to 50% emissions reduction by 2035
- Agreement includes $500 billion fund for developing nations
- Implementation begins January 2025 with yearly review process

## Historic Breakthrough

The agreement, negotiated over two intensive weeks, requires major policy shifts across industrialized nations and provides unprecedented financial support for developing countries transitioning to clean energy.

"This represents a turning point in our collective response to climate change," said UN Secretary-General António Guterres during the closing ceremony. "For the first time, we have both ambitious targets and concrete mechanisms for accountability."

The accord establishes a $500 billion fund to help developing nations adopt renewable energy technologies, triple the amount previously committed under the Paris Agreement.

## Political Challenges Ahead

While environmental groups celebrated the agreement, implementation faces significant political hurdles. The United States, EU, and China—responsible for 50% of global emissions—must pass domestic legislation to meet the targets.

"Today we made promises. Tomorrow we must keep them," warned Dr. Maria Rodriguez, Chief Climate Scientist at the UN Environmental Programme. "The real work begins with translating these commitments into national policies."

Republican lawmakers in the U.S. Congress have already signaled opposition. "We support climate action, but not at the expense of American jobs," said Senator John Mitchell (R-TX) in a statement.

## Global Reactions

Developing nations praised the financial commitments. "Finally, climate justice is becoming reality," said Kenya's Environment Minister James Ochieng. "We can't fight climate change without resources."

However, some activists argue the targets don't go far enough. Greta Thunberg, climate activist, posted on social media: "50% is progress, but science demands 65% reduction. We must keep pushing."

## What's Next

The agreement enters force January 1, 2025. Countries must submit detailed implementation plans by June 2024. An annual review process will track progress and allow adjustments.

The accord also establishes carbon pricing mechanisms and technology-sharing requirements between developed and developing nations.

## Economic Implications

The International Energy Agency estimates the transition will require $4 trillion in annual clean energy investments by 2030, up from $1.3 trillion currently.

"This is both a climate imperative and an economic opportunity," said IMF Managing Director Kristalina Georgieva. "The clean energy sector could create 30 million jobs globally by 2030."
```

## Tips for News Aggregation

When compiling news from multiple sources (like NewsAPI):

1. **Synthesize, don't copy**: Summarize in your own words
2. **Cite all sources**: Link to original articles
3. **Verify consistency**: Cross-check facts across sources
4. **Note discrepancies**: If sources disagree, mention both views
5. **Update timestamps**: Indicate when information was current
6. **Provide context**: Explain why the story matters

**Example format**:

```markdown
# Today's Top Technology News

_Compiled from multiple sources | March 7, 2024_

## AI Regulation Advances in EU Parliament

European lawmakers voted 523-46 today to advance comprehensive AI regulation, according to reports from [Reuters](link), [BBC](link), and [TechCrunch](link). The legislation would require transparency in AI systems and ban certain high-risk applications.

**Sources agree that:**

- Vote margin was overwhelming (all sources)
- Implementation timeline is 18-24 months (Reuters, BBC)
- Tech companies express concerns (TechCrunch, Reuters)

**Key perspectives:**

- EU officials: "Necessary safeguards for citizens"
- Tech industry: "Could hinder innovation"
- Privacy advocates: "Important first step"

[Continue with more stories...]
```
